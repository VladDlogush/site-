The time of sunset changes throughout the year and depends on the geographical latitude, geographical longitude and elevation above sea level observation points. In the Northern Hemisphere north of the northern tropic, the north-west quadrant is the place for sunset from the vernal equinox to the autumnal equinox (ie, from March to September), and the south-western quadrant for the rest of the year.


Sunset or sunset

In the Southern Hemisphere south of the southern tropic, the sunset from the vernal equinox to the autumnal equinox (ie, from September to March) is the south-western quadrant, and the rest of the year is the north quadrant. On the day of the , the sunset occurs almost exactly in the west for observers at all points on the Earth's surface, other than the poles.
